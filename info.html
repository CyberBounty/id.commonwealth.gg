Q: What is Commonwealth?

A global currency. Whenever a payment is made, a 10% dividend is paid proportionally and instantly to every holder. This micro-dividend is used to provide scalable, long term income stability. Points are an ERC-20 token and can be accepted and used as money.

Q: What is the max supply of Points?

There can be at most 204,939,006 (assuming all ETC possible is locked in the vault). This will never happen, and the current amount of Points grows or shrinks depending on the amount of ETC in the vault.

Q: What is the Commonwealth vault?

The vault is the true market cap of Commonwealth, it is how much Ethereum Classic is locked in the smart contract. The vault can never be changed, taken down, or modified. It is open source and has been security audited.

Q: Does the price of USD matter for Points?

The Commonwealth vault only takes in Ethereum Classic (ETC) and pays out ETC. If the price of ETC is $1,000 or $1, the system will operate the same way. If the price of ETC goes up 10x, so will the price of Points.

Q: My Points doesn't show up in my Saturn Wallet?

Commonwealth is a very new type of smart contract, which means sometimes it does not display perfectly with wallets. As long as you can see your funds on the official web wallet they are safe.

Q: What is the Rainmaker?

The Rainmaker contract is a smart contract that holds over 60,000 Points. It can never sell, but it earns dividends when others buy or sell. It provides a guaranteed price floor.
